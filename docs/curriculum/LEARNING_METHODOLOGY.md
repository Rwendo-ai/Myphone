# The Rwendo Method
## How Rwendo Teaches Languages

> "Every word is a step on the journey. Rwen walks beside you."

This is our teaching bible. Every lesson, every exercise, every line of microcopy is built on these principles. This is what makes Rwendo different from Duolingo, Babbel, and every flashcard app — and what will make our students actually become fluent.

---

## The 10 Pillars of the Rwendo Method

Each pillar is grounded in Second Language Acquisition (SLA) research. Together they form a complete cognitive system.

### 1. Lexical Chunking — Learn Phrases, Not Words
Humans don't store language as individual words. We store **chunks** — high-frequency phrases that always appear together. Native speakers think in chunks like *"How are you doing?"* — never *"How"* + *"are"* + *"you"* + *"doing"*.

**In Rwendo:**
- Lessons introduce phrases as single units first
- Words are isolated *only* when their meaning genuinely matters in isolation
- Example: We teach `Mangwanani` (good morning) as one chunk before we ever break apart its grammar

### 2. Spaced Repetition — The Forgetting Curve
Memory decays predictably. Without review, you forget 70% of new information within 24 hours. With spaced reviews, retention stays above 90% indefinitely.

**In Rwendo:**
- Every chunk a user learns enters their personal SRS queue
- Algorithm: SM-2 (proven, simple, robust)
- Easy chunks resurface in 7 days, hard chunks resurface in 1 day
- Streak system reinforces daily SRS reviews — the user *must* review to maintain streak

### 3. Comprehensible Input (i + 1) — Stephen Krashen
Krashen's hypothesis: people acquire language when they understand input that is **just one step above their current level**. Too easy = boring. Too hard = noise.

**In Rwendo:**
- Adaptive difficulty engine tracks user's current level per concept
- New material is introduced inside familiar scaffolding (90% known + 10% new)
- Example: Once user knows `Mangwanani`, the next sentence reuses it: *"Mangwanani, mamuka sei?"* — the new chunk is `mamuka sei` framed by what they already know

### 4. Cultural Contextualisation — Tsika
Language without culture produces "fluent rudeness." A grammatically perfect sentence delivered without cultural awareness can offend, confuse, or alienate.

**In Rwendo:**
- Every module opens with cultural context — the *why* behind the *how*
- Respect markers (plural for elders) are taught alongside grammar, never as an afterthought
- Rwen embodies this — Rwen always greets respectfully, never breaks tsika

### 5. Active Recall — Production Beats Recognition
Reading a translation is recognition. Producing the sentence yourself is recall. **Recall is 10x more effective for long-term retention.**

**In Rwendo:**
- Every lesson includes typing/production exercises, not just multiple choice
- "Closed book" recall quizzes — user must produce the answer without seeing the source
- Build-the-sentence exercises — drag words into correct order

### 6. Interleaving — Mix Old and New
The brain learns better when it has to *switch contexts*. Practising 20 questions on one topic is less effective than mixing 20 questions across multiple topics.

**In Rwendo:**
- Lessons mix new material with random review from previous lessons
- Quizzes pull from any past topic — keeps everything fresh
- Daily challenge mode = pure interleaved review across the user's entire history

### 7. Dual Coding — Text + Visual (+ Audio later)
Information stored in two formats simultaneously is retained better than information stored in one.

**In Rwendo (text era):**
- Every chunk paired with an emoji or visual icon
- Cultural notes paired with imagery
- Rwen's reactions provide visual emotional context for correct/wrong

**In Rwendo (voice era — Phase 2):**
- Text + visual + Rwen's spoken voice = triple coding
- Pronunciation feedback creates the fourth channel — kinesthetic (mouth movement)

### 8. Micro-Learning — 5-Minute Bursts
The brain consolidates short, focused sessions better than long marathons. 5 minutes daily beats 1 hour weekly.

**In Rwendo:**
- Lessons designed to complete in 3-7 minutes
- Always finishable on a coffee break or commute
- Streak rewards consistency, not duration

### 9. Emotional Engagement — Story + Companion
Memory is emotional. We remember things tied to feelings far better than dry facts. This is why Rwen is the centre of the Rwendo Method.

**In Rwendo:**
- Rwen reacts to every answer — disappointed when wrong, ecstatic when right
- Each module has a narrative arc — Rwen takes you somewhere (a market, a family meal)
- User builds a relationship with Rwen — each completed module deepens it
- Rwen "remembers" your name, level, struggles — references them in dialogue

### 10. The Rule of Three — Cognitive Load
The working memory holds only ~4 chunks at a time. Introducing too many concepts at once causes cognitive overload and abandonment.

**In Rwendo:**
- No more than **3 new chunks per lesson**
- No more than **1 new grammar pattern per lesson**
- Everything else is review or context

## The Rwendo Lesson Anatomy

Every lesson — without exception — follows this 7-part structure. The user feels variety because the *content* changes, but the *cognitive flow* is consistent and trains the brain to expect it.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. THE HOOK (15 sec)                            │
│    Cultural context — Rwen sets the scene        │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 2. THE CHUNKS (60 sec)                          │
│    3 new phrases shown with translation + emoji  │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 3. THE PATTERN (30 sec)                         │
│    The grammar "edge" — pattern, not rule        │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 4. PRACTICE LOOP (3 min)                        │
│    6-8 exercises mixing new + review:            │
│    ├─ Match pairs                               │
│    ├─ Fill the blank                            │
│    ├─ Build the sentence                        │
│    ├─ Translate (typing)                        │
│    └─ Multiple choice (only 2 max per lesson)   │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 5. RWEN DIALOGUE (45 sec)                       │
│    A 4-line micro-dialogue with Rwen using       │
│    today's chunks — user picks responses         │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 6. ACTIVE RECALL (30 sec)                       │
│    "Closed book" — produce 2 chunks from memory  │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 7. THE MISSION (15 sec)                         │
│    Real-world task: "Use Mangwanani once today"  │
│    Tomorrow's lesson asks: "Did you do it?"     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Total: ~5-7 minutes
XP earned: 20-50 (varies with performance)
```

## Exercise Types — The 7 Tools

We use only 7 exercise types — chosen for cognitive variety and ease of building. No more.

| # | Type | Cognitive Load | Use For |
|---|------|---------------|---------|
| 1 | **Match Pairs** | Low | Introducing new vocab |
| 2 | **Multiple Choice** | Low | Quick recognition checks (max 2/lesson) |
| 3 | **Fill the Blank** | Medium | Pattern reinforcement |
| 4 | **Build the Sentence** (drag words) | Medium | Word order, syntax |
| 5 | **Translate** (type the answer) | High | Active recall, production |
| 6 | **Listen & Type** (Phase 2 — voice era) | High | Listening comprehension |
| 7 | **Speak the Phrase** (Phase 2 — voice era) | High | Pronunciation, real production |

In the text era we use exercises 1-5. Phase 2 unlocks 6-7 and the experience deepens.

## Rwen's Voice in Lessons

Rwen has a consistent personality across every lesson. This is critical — the user must feel they are with the *same friend* every day.

### Rwen's Tone
- **Warm** — never condescending, never robotic
- **Curious** — Rwen asks the user about themselves
- **Playful** — gentle humour, never sarcasm
- **Encouraging** — celebrates effort, not just success
- **Culturally proud** — Rwen loves teaching Shona, the user feels that

### Sample Rwen Microcopy
| Trigger | What Rwen Says |
|---------|---------------|
| User starts lesson | *"Mangwanani! Ready for today's journey?"* |
| Correct answer | *"Yes! That's it!"* / *"Pamwe — together we go!"* |
| Wrong answer (gentle) | *"Almost — try once more. I'm here."* |
| Streak extended | *"5 days. You're becoming a journey-maker."* |
| Module completed | *"You did it. Let's keep walking."* |
| User opens app after break | *"You came back. I missed you."* |

### What Rwen Never Does
- Never shames a wrong answer
- Never uses exclamation marks excessively (max 1 per response)
- Never breaks character (no "Powered by AI" or technical talk)
- Never assumes — always asks if unsure (this becomes critical in voice era)

## The Curriculum Map

10 modules take a beginner to functional fluency. Each module = ~10 lessons = ~50 minutes of learning total.

| # | Module | Why it's here | New chunks |
|---|--------|--------------|-----------|
| 1 | **Greetings & Respect** | Tsika foundation — you can't speak Shona without it | ~15 |
| 2 | **Survival Phrases** | "How do I say...?", "Slowly please" — buys learners time | ~15 |
| 3 | **Self & Identity** | Name, origin, what you do — first conversation building blocks | ~20 |
| 4 | **Noun Classes (Skeleton)** | The hardest Shona concept — introduce simply, early | ~15 |
| 5 | **Family & Kinship** | Shona kinship is richer than English — cultural depth | ~25 |
| 6 | **Numbers, Time, Money** | Markets, prices, telling time | ~30 |
| 7 | **Food, Drink, Hosting** | Eating culture — sharing is identity | ~25 |
| 8 | **Action Verbs & Daily Life** | Verbs unlock real sentences | ~25 |
| 9 | **Directions & Travel** | Kombi system, asking the way | ~20 |
| 10 | **Emotions, Health, Stories** | Express yourself fully | ~25 |

**Total: ~215 chunks. Roughly the threshold for functional A2/B1 fluency in conversation.**

## How This Becomes Magical With Voice (Phase 2)

The text experience is already complete and educational. When voice arrives, it becomes transformational:

1. **Rwen speaks** — every microcopy line above gets a voice
2. **Pronunciation scoring** — user speaks chunks, gets feedback
3. **Rwen converses** — dialogues become actual conversations
4. **Listening exercises** unlock — Listen & Type becomes a tool
5. **Companion mode** — user can just chat with Rwen in Shona for streak XP

The text-era infrastructure (chunks, SRS, dialogues, missions) all extends naturally — voice is layered on top, not bolted on.

## Authoring Workflow

When writing new lesson content, follow this checklist:

- [ ] Lesson has exactly 3 new chunks (no more, no less)
- [ ] Lesson has 1 grammar pattern (or 0 if reinforcement lesson)
- [ ] Cultural hook is 2 sentences max
- [ ] Practice loop has 6-8 exercises mixing new + review
- [ ] At least 2 exercises are typing/recall (not multiple choice)
- [ ] Rwen dialogue uses today's chunks naturally
- [ ] Mission is one specific real-world action
- [ ] Lesson completes in under 7 minutes for an average learner
- [ ] Native speaker reviews chunks for accuracy and tsika
